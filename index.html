campus life

In Grass Valley you begin school at 8:30 am (kids who arrive early can play on the playground whilst they wait). Once you are in the school you head to your (anywhere from kindergarden to fifth grade) homeroom, which would be math and science or English language arts. After lunch, classes are swapped. On normal days you get out of school at 2:45 but on Wednesdays you get out at 12:30. For advanced students every thursday get an hour of advanced stem learning including advanced math, coding robots or games, building robots, and much much more. Every month (it is random within the month) we will have a surprise fire drill as well to stay safe.

Alice Cotter

Alice Cotter

Alice Miller Cotter, graduate of the Grass Valley School District, received her Bachelor of Arts in music from the University of California, Berkeley and her Master of Arts in music from Princeton University. Currently, Alice is completing a Doctorate in music history at Princeton, where her scholarship focuses on the ethics and politics of musical responses to war. She is expanding her research into a book and plans to teach music history and theory at the college level. Alice considers her years in the Grass Valley School District as among the most intellectually formative of her early education.
About Alice Cotter
